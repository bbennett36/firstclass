First Class Travelers LLC. Motor Coach Operator Job Description

As a Motor Coach Operator for First Class Travelers LLC. You will ALWAYS display a High Level of Safety and Professionalism.

Duties:

Motor coach drivers operate 40 to 45’ long buses or other company vehicles to transport passengers for scheduled runs, trips and tours. Drivers must follow predetermined routes to collect and drop off passengers. Drivers are responsible for the safety of the passengers and must observe all traffic laws. As a driver you will also serve as customer service representatives. The job requires drivers to operate vehicles for several hours, depending on how long the scheduled is. Drivers inspect the coach for safety and ensure that maintenance is performed at scheduled intervals. Coach drivers also maintain records.

Work Environment

Drivers operate large vehicles on congested city streets and or over long stretches of highway. The job requires physical strength. Fatigue is a major safety factor among all drivers, who must stay alert and aware of their limits. Operators drive in 4 seasons of weather, including rain and snow. Drivers may face unruly or intoxicated passengers. Dealing with passenger complaints is common. The job often takes operators away from home for long hours or for days.

Electro-mechanical technicians combine knowledge of mechanical technology with knowledge of electrical and electronic circuits. They install, troubleshoot, repair, and upgrade electronic and computer-controlled mechanical systems, such as robotic assembly machines

Duties:

Electro-mechanical technicians typically do the following:

  • Read schematics, and diagrams to determine the method and sequence of assembly of a part, machine, or piece of equipment.
  • Verify dimensions of parts, using precision measuring instruments, to ensure that specifications are met
  • Operate metalworking machines to make housings, fittings, and fixtures
  • Repair and calibrate hydraulic and pneumatic assemblies
  • Test the performance of electro-mechanical assemblies, using test instruments
  • Install electronic parts and hardware, using soldering equipment and hand tools

Electro-mechanical technicians solve technical problems in manufacturing, research and development, construction, inspection, sales and maintenance using the principles and theories of science, mathematics and engineering. In quality control, electro-mechanical technicians inspect processes and products, perform tests and gather data. Electro-mechanical technicians set up or build equipment, perform experiments, gather data and record results or help engineers and scientists in other ways. Using computer-aided design and drafting (CADD) equipment, they also assist in design work.

Typically, electro-mechanical technicians perform similar tasks as engineers, combining understanding of mechanical engineering technology with that of electrical and electronic circuits. They create, develop, test and build computer-controlled and electronic mechanical systems. For example, they may work wheelchair lifts assemblies, or they may operate these machines in other worksites and factories. Their works sometimes overlaps that of mechanical engineering technicians and electronic engineering technicians.

TA53-6061 Travel Attendant North Jersey 8 NO Full time Open

First Class Travelers LLC. Travel Attendant Job Description

First Class Travelers Travel Attendants provide services to ensure the safety and comfort of passengers on it buses.

The T/A has a very important position.

Duties:

  • Announce safety and emergency procedures such as the use of emergency hatches and windows in the motor coach.
  • Answer passengers' questions about the trip, weather, travel routes and services, arrival times, and/or schedules, collecting tickets.
  • Assist passengers in placing carry-on luggage, garment in overhead compartments, or under-seats.
  • Assist passengers while entering or disembarking the bus.
  • Know emergency procedures, crew coordination, length of travel time,
  • Offer food and or beverage services to a number of passengers.
  • Check to ensure that food, beverages, reading material, emergency equipment, and other supplies are aboard and are in adequate supply.
  • Conduct periodic trips through the bus to ensure passenger comfort, and to distribute reading material, as well as security checks through-out the bus cabin.
  • Determine special assistance needs of passengers such as small children, the elderly, unaccompanied minor or disabled persons.
  • Cleaning the inside of bus in between trips. Putting the seat backs in the up-right position, placing the arm rests in the down position, picking up trash from floor and seats.
  • Direct passengers in case of emergency

The primary job of T/A’s is to keep passengers safe and to ensure that everyone follows security regulations and laws. T/A’s also try to make the trip comfortable and enjoyable for passengers. A T/A’s most important responsibility, however, is to help passengers in the event of an emergency. This responsibility ranges from dealing with unruly passengers to, fighting fires, and directing evacuations.

T/As work primarily in the cabin of the bus except when collecting tickets, assisting passengers entering, or disembarking the bus. T/A’s will deal directly with the public and standing for long periods. Occasionally T/A’s will deal with traffic conditions, which can make providing service more difficult and cause preforming your job a little more harder.

All T/A’s work full time, but they usually have variable schedules. T/A’s often work nights, weekends, and holidays because First Class Travelers operate 7 days per week. A typical on-duty shift is usually about 10 to 11 hours per day. However Traffic, weather and unpredictable condition will play as the main factor in those situations.

  • Attentiveness. T/A’s must be aware of passengers’ needs to ensure a pleasant travel experience. They must also be aware of any security or safety risks.
  • Communication skills. T/A’s should speak clearly, listen attentively, and interact comfortably with passengers and other crew members.
  • Customer-service skills. T/A’s should have poise, tact, and resourcefulness to handle stressful situations and meet passengers' needs.
  • Decision-making skills. T/A’s must be able to act decisively in emergency situations.
  • Physical stamina. T/A’s may need to lift baggage and stand and walk for long periods.

T/A’s should present a professional appearance and not have visible tattoos, body piercings, or an unusual hairstyle or makeup.

All T/A’s must have at least a high school diploma or the equivalent. Prospective T/A’s need previous work experience in customer service. Applicants must be at least 24 years old, eligible to work in the United States, and pass a background check.
